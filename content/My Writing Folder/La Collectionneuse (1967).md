Rohmer's first colour feature, and it’s a sumptuous one. A summer retreat as filmed by Néstor Almendros becomes a golden-hued arena of persistent chatter and passive aggression, its St. Tropez humidity and pellucid lakes seeping through the frames as palpably as the sexual tension. Rest and relaxation is impossible for the over-active mind; so it goes for prickly artist Daniel (Daniel Pommereulle) and wannabe gallery owner Adrian (Patrick Bauchau) who fall into obsessive distraction with the waif-like Haydée (Haydée Politoff), the third guest at the country house and a ‘collector’ of men. Once you accept Rohmer’s sympathises with her and not the calculating males, the funnier it gets to hear Adrian muse lines like "I intended to test the soundness of my hypothesis." A string of dandyish affectations - lots of drooping in chairs, like aristocrats too weary to work - concealing inner disquiet, culminating not with a dramatic revelation but in dissatisfied resignation.
