Richard Widmark as weaselly tout Harry Fabian, "an artist without an art”, racing through the seedier sides of London, an image of sweaty despair and an immersive city tour. Jules Dassin never read Gerald Kersh's book of the same name, yet the glimpses into the lives of club owners and peddlers and counterfeiters drawn with pudgy British faces are nevertheless rich in novelistic detail. Everybody’s scheming against everybody else; Harry just can't get ahead as his desperation is too stark, his American eagerness too much for a world so English and gray. Shot on location to dodge the HUAC hearings, Dassin the exiled director navigates the city with a deftness his own exiled protagonist lacks, its war-torn buildings and forgotten backstreets forming into harsh mazes just as Harry’s spinning plates all come crashing down.
